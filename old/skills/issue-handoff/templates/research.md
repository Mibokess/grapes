# Research Handoff Template

The next agent will investigate a question or explore a problem. Give them a focused scope and everything you already know so they don't retrace your steps.

## Plan Structure

```markdown
## Linear Issue
[Issue ID and title]

## Research Questions
1. [Specific question to answer]
2. [Specific question to answer]

## What I Already Know

### Investigated
- `path/to/file.py` — what I looked at, what I found
- `path/to/other.py` — what I looked at, what I found
- [Doc or source] — key findings

### Findings So Far
- [Finding 1 — verified fact]
- [Finding 2 — verified fact]
- [Hypothesis — clearly marked as unverified]

## What Hasn't Been Investigated
- `path/to/unexplored.py` — why it matters, what to look for
- [Area/topic] — why it's relevant, suggested approach

## Suggested Approach
1. [Start here — why]
2. [Then look at this — why]
3. [Then check this — why]

## Scope & Boundaries
- **In scope**: [What to investigate]
- **Out of scope**: [What to skip and why]
- **Stop when**: [What "done" looks like for this research]

## Expected Output
- [What the findings should look like]
- [Where to post them — Linear comment format, e.g. `[FINDINGS]`]
- [What decisions the findings should inform]
```

## Checklist

1. **Research questions** are specific and answerable
2. **What's known** clearly separates verified facts from hypotheses
3. **What's investigated** lists exact files/sources so the next agent skips them
4. **What's uninvestigated** explains why each area matters
5. **Suggested approach** gives a concrete starting point
6. **Scope** has clear boundaries — the agent knows when to stop
7. **Expected output** describes what "done" looks like
8. **No vague directions** ("look into the codebase", "explore options")
9. **A fresh agent can start investigating immediately**
